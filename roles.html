VP Students

Roles & Responsibilities

An overview of what the VP Students does, the key areas of responsibility, and committee involvement within the GSS.

The role

What the VP Students Does

The VP Students manages graduate student programs and services — including budgeting, social events, advocacy, and collaborations with Affiliate Organizations and partners across UBC.

🎉
Social Events

Community & Events

Plans and organizes social events, programs, workshops, and classes for graduate students across UBC, plus networking opportunities with the broader grad community.

🤝
Partnerships

Affiliate Organizations

Manages relationships with GSS Affiliate Organizations, coordinates funding applications, event promotion through GSS channels, and collaborative opportunities.

📋
Administration

Programs & Services

Oversees budgeting and delivery of graduate student programs and services, including the AO funding pool of $1,000 per organization per academic year.

📣
Advocacy

Student Advocacy

Advocates for graduate student interests and ensures graduate student voices are represented in planning events and programs that serve the wider community.

Committees

Committee Involvement

The VP Students works alongside the following GSS committees. All committee meetings are open to current UBC graduate students.

Elections & Engagement Committee

Manages the Society's referendums and annual elections, plans social events, programs, workshops, classes, and networking opportunities for grad students.

Biweekly / Monthly

Academic & External Affairs Committee

Lobbies important academic and personal issues to the University and externally, and conducts research such as the annual GSS Student Satisfaction Survey.

Bi-monthly

House Finance Committee

Directs and manages the financial wellbeing of the Society and oversees the use of facilities at the Graduate Student Centre at Thea Koerner House.

Biweekly

Human Resources Committee

Ensures the Society is staffed with a strong team, monitors staffing needs, and oversees staff recruitment and performance evaluations.

Bimonthly

Governance & Accountability Committee

Engages in strategic planning, reviews the governance structure of the Society, and oversees the performance of all other committees.

Bimonthly

Indigenous Engagements Ad Hoc Committee

Provides culturally-relevant recommendations to the GSS Council to promote indigeneity, Indigenous representation, equity, and reconciliation within the Society.

Bimonthly

GSS Council

Council Role

The VP Students participates in GSS Council, the primary governing body made up of elected departmental representatives, the executive team, and graduate student senators.

Monthly Council Meetings

Council meets on the third Thursday of every month in the Michael Kingsmill Forum (Room 4301) in the AMS Student Nest, from 5:30 – 7:30 pm. All meetings are open to graduate students to attend.
